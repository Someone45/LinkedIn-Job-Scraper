Desc
If you love digital products, want to see your work online, and have a passion for thinking ahead of the rest, we’d love to be the next stop on your career journey.

Code and Theory’s Remote Internship Program is ideal for rising college seniors, recent grads, and career changers who are looking for hands-on experience at a digital-first creative agency.
We are now accepting applications for our remote, 12 week, paid internship program starting September 12, 2022 across many of our departments.

Our Production team is responsible for managing the day-to-day work activities for two or three concurrent client engagements. They leverage the expertise and knowledge of a multi-disciplinary team of strategists, designers, and technologists to deliver a successful product on time and budget.

As a Production team member, you will work closely with client counterparts to make sure that work is conveyed clearly and progresses through to launch. Clients trust Production’s knowledge, management of projects, and insight on the project’s progress. You have strong presentation skills and are actively developing new skills to make sure the work is launched efficiently and with creative excellence.

Eligibility
You can be entering your senior year of undergraduate studies (graduating Spring 2023), a recent graduate, master's student, career changer, or reentering the workforce after taking some time off
You must be currently based in the U.S.

What’s required to apply
A resume
Complete the following prompt in 200 words or less: At Code and Theory, we believe what we make says a lot about who we are. Please tell us about something you created or a project you worked on and why it's meaningful to you.
Tell us if you are available for 40 hours per week. If not 40 hours, what is your availability?

What you’ll do
Manage organization and internal team tracking for reviews, feedback, and asset tracking and management
Use products like Google Drive, DropBox, Basecamp, and other asset management systems
Interface with all team members and participants on a daily and weekly basis.
Work with the Creative and Technology teams to:
Check in on bandwidth/capacity
Manage/report/status on asset development progress
Identify staffing needs (resource allocations)
Help with preliminary estimating for daily Design/Technology tasks
Participate in client meetings with producers and manage meeting notes, file delivery, and other administrative client related asks as directed by the Senior Production staff
Manage project timelines and budgets set by Senior Production staff
Work with Producers to create SOWs and contracts for clients and vendors
Manage client level account maintenance tasks: hard cost estimates, purchase orders, freelancer invoicing, timesheet management/approval, etc.
